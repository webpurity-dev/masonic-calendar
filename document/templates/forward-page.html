The size and scope of the Dorset Masonic Calendar has naturally grown and widened over 235 years of the development of the Masonic Province of Dorset, from a minor document, barely one quarter of an inch thick in earlier times, to its present substantial form of today. The publication contains a comprehensive wealth of information and reference, presented in a clear and concise format, and is made available to all members of the Province whatever their station.

The Calendar records the creation and progress of all of our 49 Lodges, (plus Royal Arch Chapters and other Orders) and lists, for ongoing reference and interest, the succession of the Provincial Executive, Grand Officers and Provincial and private Lodge Appointments year on year, together with detailed information of membership, meeting dates, locations and other important matters pertinent to each private Lodge and individual Member.

Researchers, and Brethren with an interest in reviewing the roots and history of their Lodges and Fellows, will be able to plot their own individual Masonic careers, together with those of other individuals, from their first entrance into the Order, details of their progress through the various Offices of the Lodge to the Worshipful Master’s Chair and subsequent elevation, where appropriate, to Provincial and/or Grand Rank. Each page contains detailed information, readily available as a current source of reference and reminder, or as an evolving record of the long and detailed history of our Province.

In short, this series of volumes may be used in conjunction with other Provincial documents to widen each Brother’s awareness of his Lodge, his Province as well as his predecessors and contemporaries.

Such a publication, updated as it is in detail each year, cannot be of true value unless it contains accurate and reliable information. If any individual by chance discovers any item of doubt or error, especially if relevant to his own personal details and Masonic career, it is hoped that he will take the trouble to contact the Editor, using the details supplied within, to advise him of the need for adjustment or correction. Only in this way can we be absolutely sure that the ever-growing records contained within the complete set of volumes remains faithful to a true representation and record of this Province.

In commending these pages to you as a ready and easily portable source of Page 10 Provincial Grand Lodge of Dorset reference and information, I would also draw to your special attention our very comprehensive Provincial website at www.dorset-freemasonry.org which, likewise, contains a wealth of pertinent and valuable information. The web-site, being updated on a regular monthly basis, provides additional news and detail of interest, not necessarily to be found in the Calendar. Autumn 2014 saw the birth of Dorset’s adventure into Social Media by the creation of Facebook and Twitter accounts. Although only a few months old, our pages are proving to be popular, widely viewed and highly successful. On-going news from Lodges detailing events, special occasions and any other activity is essential to maintain a fresh stream of postings.

Each Lodge has been requested to appoint a “media officer” for this purpose. Make sure your Lodge is featured and seen! Comments, statements and articles of any kind sent to media@dorsetfreemasonry.info will be welcomed. Your Lodge’s profile will be enhanced in these important areas of communication. No article should be considered too small or too trivial. Every item of news will be of value in projecting the image of the Province of Dorset to members and non-members alike.

This annual publication cannot be achieved without a great deal of dedication and industry on the part of those responsible for its creation. I therefore take this opportunity to record my admiration and grateful thanks to the Editor, WBro Matthew Burt, PPrGSwdB, and to all those who assist him so greatly with the timely submission of the information necessary to allow him to co-ordinate and present it in its orderly and user-friendly form. May this year’s Dorset Calendar be of interest and value to you all, and may you also continue to enjoy your Freemasonry to the full, in whatever direction that may take you during 2015.
